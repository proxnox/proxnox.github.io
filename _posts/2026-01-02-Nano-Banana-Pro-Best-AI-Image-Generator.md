## Common Limitations and AI Hallucinations

Despite its impressive capabilities, nano banana comes with several limitations you should be aware of when using this AI tool. Let's examine where this powerful AI image generator sometimes falls short.

### Incorrect Infographic Data in Product Visuals

One of the most concerning issues with nano banana pro is its tendency to fabricate information in data-driven visuals. My testing revealed that when creating infographics or product comparisons, the AI often invents statistics and specifications that look completely plausible but are entirely false.

For example, when asked to create iPhone comparison infographics, the AI couldn't correctly render what the back of any iPhone 17 looked like, instead producing images resembling earlier mock-ups. Even after being explicitly directed to use information from a specific source, the **AI still created visualizations filled with inaccurate data**.

This problem is especially dangerous because:


- The fabricated data appears visually convincing

- Without subject expertise, you might not notice the errors

- The incorrect information could be shared as factual


Google acknowledges this limitation, advising users to **"always verify the factual accuracy of data-driven visuals like diagrams and infographics"**.

### Inconsistent Character Rendering in Group Images

If you're creating multiple images featuring the same characters, nano banana gemini sometimes struggles with consistency. In group scenes particularly, characters may display subtle changes in facial features or other identifying elements between generations.

This issue becomes especially noticeable when trying to create a series of images for a project that requires visual continuity. As noted by users, the tool **"fails to maintain 100% consistency with the original photo"**  across multiple edits.

### Aspect Ratio Reversion in Original Model

A persistent frustration with google nano banana involves aspect ratio handling. The original model frequently ignores requested aspect ratios, defaulting to square (1:1) images regardless of specifications. This issue occurs most commonly when:


- No strong visual reference exists

- Aspect ratio is only mentioned in text

- The model tries to stay "safe" with standard sizes 


Many users report that even after successful edits, the image often appears "stretched," "squished," or "shifted in space" compared to the original. Understandably, this makes blending generated elements with existing images difficult.

### Blurring Issues in High-Resolution Outputs

While gemini 3 nano banana pro can generate higher-resolution images, these outputs occasionally suffer from quality issues. Users report that **high-resolution images (2K/4K)** sometimes appear blurrier than expected.

First, the tool struggles with rendering small text and fine details accurately. Second, after multiple edits, image quality tends to degrade noticeably. As one user explained, images become "both overly smoothed and weirdly dithered at the same time" after just a few sequential edits.

This quality degradation is especially problematic with the reflection removal feature. Although nano banana ai can remove reflections more effectively than the original model, "it still compromised the fine details and distorted the faces"  in the process.

The conversational editing flow, though convenient, becomes less useful when "your image starts getting worse after each edit" , forcing you to start over rather than iteratively improve your creation.

## AI Watermarking and Trust in Generated Images

Ever wondered if you can trust what you're seeing from nano banana? Trust in AI-generated images has become a critical concern, primarily as these tools get better at creating realistic visuals.

### SynthID Invisible Watermark Detection

Every image created with nano banana pro contains **SynthID—Google's imperceptible digital watermark**. Unlike visible marks, SynthID embeds itself directly into image pixels , remaining invisible to human eyes yet detectable by specialized software. Remarkably, this technology has already watermarked over 10 billion pieces of content.

To verify if an image came from google nano banana, simply:


- Upload it to the Gemini app

- Ask: "Was this generated by Google AI?"

- The app detects the embedded SynthID watermark 


### Limitations of Visual Watermark Removal

Nano banana gemini also applies a visible "Gemini sparkle" watermark in the corner of images. Yet this visible mark can easily be cropped out.

The invisible SynthID watermark, meanwhile, survives common modifications including:


- Cropping and resizing


- Adding filters


- Compression 


### Gemini's AI Policy on Uploaded Content

For professional work requiring clean visuals, gemini 3 nano banana pro removes visible watermarks for Google AI Ultra subscribers. Interestingly, these premium-tier images still retain invisible SynthID watermarking.

Given that watermarking techniques aren't standardized across AI systems, verification only works within Google's ecosystem—SynthID can't identify images from non-Google models.
